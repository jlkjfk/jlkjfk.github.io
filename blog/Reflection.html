Reflection on Foundations

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Through practice the growth mindset and meditation, I can now keep optimistic under pressure and have a positive attitude to all the workload.
At the first two weeks I tend to compare my learning progress to others, and being frustrated when I'm falling behind. I realised the negative emotion makes me can’t focus on my study and decrease efficiency.
I tend to do a lot of research into the topics before trying it out, which sometimes waste time as I can’t remember all the new knowledge when I try to recall them.
It also confuse me when I am coding as there’s so many unlinked ideas in my brain, but I can’t work out the logic between them.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Neuroplasticity and growth mindset are the most surprising part.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found reflection and writing the weekly blogs are the most challenging aspects. I tried to avoid leave perfunctory remarks, but sometimes it’s really difficult to write in details.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

As stated in the handbook: "Tech employers aren't looking for top grades or the person with the most outstanding code. They're looking for exceptional human beings who will enhance the culture of their team".
Soft skills play a critical role in the workplace, the ones who have good soft skills are more likely to be successful.
Programming is a type of profession where we need good teamwork and communication skills. Critical thinking ability helps us to look at a problem from multiple perspectives using the available information to get a solution that covers different aspects of the issue.
Learning to be self-aware and grounded through the core study, we have the opportunities to learn ourself and develop a positive mindset, which helps us to handle pressure.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I really appreciate the core learning which is a great way to learn about myself, to think about something I never thought, and to develop a growth mindset.
I may either give up or being extremely frustrated without the core learning, and telling myself that programming is not for me. By practice growth mindset, I can now hear a voice from my head saying “I just can’t do it yet.
Don’t compare to others, keep learning and practice, a small improvements everyday will lead to huge results.”
